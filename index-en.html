Synopsis


This comic is inspired by the fascinating coexistence between the famous playwright Lope de Vega and Captain Alonso de Contreras in the writer's house in 1624. The work combines adventure and literature, exploring the friendship between two emblematic figures of the Golden Age.

Lope de Vega, known as the "Phoenix of Wits", is one of the most important figures in universal literature, with a prolific work that revolutionized Spanish theater, providing it with psychological depth and a unique style.

For his part, Alonso de Contreras is remembered as a bold soldier and narrator of his own life, whose memoirs offer a vivid portrait of the era. This project not only pays tribute to Lope's literary wit, but also to the historical richness of Spain.

The authors, Jorge Esteban (Jorgiot) and Juan Torres (El Torres), present a work that combines historical rigor with visual creativity, rescuing a little-known episode that underlines the link between literature and real experiences. Thus, the comic becomes a gateway to both the artistic and cultural world, inviting us to explore the Spain of the Golden Age from an innovative and accessible perspective.



The authors


El Torres


Juan Antonio Torres (born in Malaga in 1972) is a renowned Spanish comic book writer, specializing in horror and adventure. His career began in the 90s, when he directed editorial projects at MegaMultimedia and later founded his own label, Sulaco Ediciones. Later he created Amigo Comics to focus on the American market and is currently part of the editorial project Karras. His works have been published by international publishers such as IDW Publishing and Image Comics. Among his most notable works are "Nancy in Hell", "The Veil", and "The Suicide Forest", the latter of which has won three Haxtur Awards. Her work "El Fantasma de Gaudí" earned her awards such as the Expocómic for Best National Screenwriter in 2015 and Best National Work at the Barcelona Comic Fair 2016. She was also nominated for the prestigious Eisner and Harvey Awards. Other notable titles include "Camisa de Fuerza", "Goya: Lo sublime terrible" and "Roman Ritual". El Torres has received numerous awards, such as the Carlos Giménez Award for Best National Screenwriter (2017) and the José Sanchís Grau Award (2018). In addition, her narrative has been acclaimed both for its creativity and for its ability to connect with international audiences.

Jorge Esteban Urabayen (Jorgiot)


Jorge is an artist linked to the world of comics, known especially for his work as a colorist. He has collaborated on works such as "Paredes, la leyenda", a historical comic about the adventurer Diego García de Paredes, and also on international projects such as "Black Demon Tales", or “Killswitch” published by Scout Comics, where he worked with creative teams focused on genre and adventure stories. In addition, he has participated in titles such as "Giallo", which mixes elements of horror and mystery, published by Leviathan Labs, "Alcatraz, de la A a la Z" a collective work created during the 2020 lockdown, and is preparing several projects such as “Phantasmagoria2” and “Por este hacha”, which will be released in 2025, in collaboration with the scriptwriter El Torres and which will be published by Karras Comics.

Cascaborra Publisher


Cascaborra Ediciones is a Spanish publishing house specialising in historical comics. Founded with the aim of bringing the History of Spain closer to the general public, its most notable collection is "Historia de España en viñetas" (History of Spain in cartoons), which combines historical rigour with the visual appeal of comics. Since its inception in 2016, the publishing house has published works on emblematic episodes such as the Battle of El Rif, the Spanish Tercios or the figure of Goya, managing to capture the attention of both comic book fans and teachers who use its titles as educational tools in the classroom. With nearly 50 published titles and plans to expand the collection to more than 100 works, Cascaborra has firmly opted for Spanish authors, many of them debutants, as part of its commitment to national talent. His work has been highlighted for its ability to disseminate complex historical topics in an accessible and entertaining way, contributing to the rescue of key episodes and figures in Spanish history for new generations.

For more information, visit his official website.

Historical Context


The comic "Espada y Pluma" is set in the Spanish Golden Age, an era of artistic and literary splendor where geniuses such as Lope de Vega, Miguel de Cervantes and Francisco de Quevedo coexisted. The story is based on the memoirs of Alonso de Contreras, a captain of the Tercios de Flandes, who in his book Discurso de mi vida recounts his adventures as a soldier, privateer and traveler, as well as his encounters with great figures of the time. The work delves into the relationship between Contreras and Lope de Vega, highlighting the humanity of these historical figures in an environment full of intrigue, wars and intellectual debates. Through the comic, the authors highlight the cultural richness of this era while presenting a story full of action, humor and reflections on life and mortality. This carefully researched setting is mixed with the resources of historical comics, combining narrative rigor and evocative art that transports the reader to the vibrant universe of the 17th century. The Cascaborra Ediciones publishing house is known for this educational and visually appealing approach in its publications on the history of Spain.
